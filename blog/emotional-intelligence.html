Emotional Intelligence (EQ or EI)

What is Emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EI) or emotional quotient (EQ) is the measurement of a person’s social and emotional intelligence. It can indicate their ability to view, understand and manage the emotions within themselves and during interactions with others. There are several factors that make up a person’s EQ, these include:

• Creating self-awareness by viewing your emotions, without judgment, and the impact the have on yourself and others.

• Managing your emotions, avoiding snap emotional decisions or actions and handling stress.

How is EQ different to IQ?

While EQ and IQ are important to success they measure different aspects of a human’s intelligence.

IQ measures a person’s cognitive intelligence. Determining their ability to think logically, solve problems and understand complicated ideas.

EQ on the other hand, measures a person’s emotional and social intelligence. It indicates their ability to manage emotions within themself and during interactions with others.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it is a big part of success in life both personally and professionally. Your emotional intelligence is not set and can be developed over time. This will help you navigate and manage social and emotional situations.

Increasing your EQ can have a positive effect on many aspects of your personal life, such as; Relationships, mental health, stress management, and overall happiness.

looking at a higher EQ in a professional setting, it can also improve: job performance, your ability to work with or lead a team and your problem solving / conflict resolution skills.
